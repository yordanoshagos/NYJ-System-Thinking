Iceberg Module: The Transformation of Mama Mboga

Events

In recent years, 80% of shoppers spend most of their time online, leading to a shift from physical to online shopping. Mama Mboga, the cornerstone of Kenya’s food markets, now sees an opportunity in going digital. This change is fueled by customer pain points like long wait times, dirty hands from selecting produce, and limited late-hour access to vegetables.

Despite being underrepresented, Mama Mboga is crucial to Kenya’s economy, handling production, distribution, and consumption with no government aid. Challenges like fires at timber kiosks, early morning hustles, and cash security show the need for new digital interventions.

Patterns and Trends

Though fraud detection is weak, Kenya's e-commerce sector is expanding rapidly. A new digital economy blueprint and the widespread use of M-Pesa make online transactions easier. According to UNCTAD, Kenya ranks 3rd in Africa's e-commerce markets after South Africa and Nigeria.

In 2024, Kenya’s e-commerce revenue is projected to hit $900 million, with over 12 million users. Shoppers show most interest in clothing, shoes, and food & beverages. They primarily use search engines (63%), customer reviews (58%), and social media (51%) to gather product info.

Mental Models

Two main mental models shape this shift. Customers believe online shopping addresses pain points like time, cost, and choice. On the other hand, Mama Mbogas are hesitant, believing digital adoption is hard, though they aim to meet customer needs and increase profits.

To remain profitable, Mama Mboga must embrace customer feedback and integrate digital tools. There's now a movement among Mama Mbogas to slowly adopt technology, strengthen customer engagement, and ultimately boost business performance.
